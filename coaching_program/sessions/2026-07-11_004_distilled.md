## What this session settled

**Program goal (first time stated):** aesthetic physique "for the female gaze" —
definition learned over time, not assumed up front.

**Phase 2 doctrine (locked):**
- Recomp (simultaneous fat loss + muscle gain) is the *target and optimal path*
  for a padded novice — but the *executable* plan installs **one skill at a
  time** because adherence is the binding constraint. **Food first, gym later.**
- Keep it easy; least friction wins; fat loss starts in the kitchen; expose
  changes slowly and incrementally.

**GM → Phase 2 handoff (decided this session):** **No gap.** The moment the GM
gate (G1–G4) clears, **Brick One is stacked immediately.** Graduation itself is
the trigger.

**Food-brick advancement rule (decided this session — the core new mechanic):**
- Each food brick is a **weekly binary task**: did he do it this week, yes or no?
- **One clean week is enough** → stack the next brick the following week.
- Fail the week → **hold and repeat** the same brick; do not advance.
- Every step stacks on the last; nothing moves until the current one is done.
- Deliberately *simpler* than the GM gate. Rationale: GM installed the meta-habit
  of showing up (needed durability proof — tenure/autonomy/anchoring). A food
  brick is a concrete weekly task, so a plain binary suffices — trading
  durability rigor for speed and simplicity.

**Brick One (from the handoff, carried into the plan):** install plain 0% Greek
yogurt, week one, nothing else changes. Sourcing: unflavored, ingredients =
milk + live cultures only (auto-screens sugar-loaded imposters); large tub; lock
the brand he likes. Task: eat the whole tub by end of week; toppings unrestricted
during adoption. Verify: ingredient photo before, empty-tub photo after. Baseline
capture: brand kept + toppings used (feeds the future swap phase). Done-bar: tub
emptied + correct product confirmed by photo. Adoption, not optimization.
