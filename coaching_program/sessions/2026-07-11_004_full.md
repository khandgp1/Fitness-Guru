Coaching Session — Next Phase (Post-GM) — 2026-07-11

Purpose

Design the phase that begins after the client graduates the GM ritual. Overall program goal: aesthetic physique for the female gaze — definition to be learned over time, not assumed. This session scopes the first post-GM move.

Positions established

Client model: skinny-fat / padded male, early training age, limited history of hard eating or hard training.

Strategic debate — recomp vs. sequential skills:
- Coach (guru) POV, evidence-backed: for a padded novice, recomposition (lose fat + build muscle simultaneously) is optimal — muscle-building ROI is highest now, not lowest. "Cut first, build later" only holds for advanced lifters. "Toned" is not a tissue state — it's muscle + low body fat; leanness is the gate, muscle is the payoff. "Leaner partitions better" is true but an inverted-U (best window ~10–15% BF), a second-order lever behind training stimulus and protein.
- Client POV: most men lack the discipline/competency to learn eating and training at once. Install one skill at a time. Food first, gym second.
- Resolution: Recomp stays the target and the ideal. But the executable plan sequences skills one at a time because adherence is the binding constraint. Food first, gym next — on a defined trigger (proven food-habit adherence), never open-ended. The recomp window has a clock; the gym gets an on-ramp, not an indefinite delay.

Doctrine (locked)

- Skills installed one at a time. Food first, gym next on a defined trigger — never open-ended.
- Recomp is the target and optimal path; skills are sequenced to reach it, not run simultaneously.
- Philosophy: keep it easy. Least friction wins. Fat loss starts in the kitchen (diet is the bigger, more controllable lever than exercise). Adherence is the whole game. Expose changes slowly and incrementally.

Brick One — Week One after GM

- Single skill: install plain 0% Greek yogurt. Nothing else changes.
- Sourcing spec: any brand, unflavored, minimally processed, no added sugar. Ingredient list must read milk + live cultures only (this auto-screens "vanilla/honey" imposters hiding 10–15g added sugar). Buy a large multi-serving tub. Try different brands; lock the one he genuinely likes — adherence lives there.
- Task: eat the whole tub by end of week. Toppings unrestricted during adoption (friction reduction is the priority; a single ~530-cal tub can't damage the not-yet-running deficit).
- Verify: label/ingredient photo before start; empty tub photo at week's end. Trust, then verify.
- Baseline capture: client reports brand kept + toppings used — data for the future swap phase, not a restriction.
- Success bar: tub emptied + correct product confirmed by photo. Adoption, not optimization.

Supporting facts (evidence-backed)

- Plain 0% Greek yogurt: ~17–18g protein per ~100 cal — elite protein-to-calorie ratio. A 32oz/907g tub ≈ ~90g protein for ~530 cal. Cheap, no prep, satiating. On its own ~13g protein/day averaged — a foundation brick, not the whole plan.
- Habit-stacking one easy food at a time is consistent with behavior-change evidence: simple additions automate faster than complex changes; addition beats restriction for adherence.
- Toppings are the real leak long-term (50g granola ≈ +220 cal for little protein). Handled by incremental swaps later, not restriction now.

Open threads (next session)

- GM graduation bar — deferred by client; confirm the GM→brick-one handoff has no gap before go-live.
- Define the trigger that moves the client from food skill to gym skill.
- Brick two + the incremental topping-swap sequence (baseline data drives the first, highest-leverage swap).
- Client stats needed to convert doctrine into numbers (bodyweight, target protein, maintenance calories, deficit size, lactose tolerance, training access).

Constraints noted

- Guru operates through two specialists only (nutrition, resistance training); all prescriptions are evidence-sourced.
- Not a medical provider — injuries, health conditions, or disordered-eating flags go to a qualified clinician.
