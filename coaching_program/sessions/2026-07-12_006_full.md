> # The Food Ladder: Brick Two → Recomp
>
> ## Governing logic
> Every rung is a behavior, never a number. The deficit is never announced — it's engineered to appear as a side effect of skills the client thinks are about *adding* good food, not removing calories. Restriction-flavored rungs sit last, only after satiety scaffolding makes them easy. Nothing is ever dropped; each brick stacks and keeps running.
>
> **One assumption stated up front, load-bearing:** this ladder produces fat loss + muscle *gain* only if resistance training is running in parallel. Food alone gets you fat loss + muscle *retention*. Recomp is real for this exact population — untrained, higher body fat — and it fades as the client leans out and gains gym experience. Do not sell it as producing both forever.
>
> ## The rungs
>
> | # | Task (what the client is told) | Done-bar (weekly binary) | Why it sits here |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | **B1** *(exists)* | Buy a tub of plain 0% Greek yogurt, toppings free, empty it by week's end. | Tub empty? | Installs buying + eating a high-protein whole food. Zero restriction. |
> | **B2 — Protein anchor, one meal** | Build one meal a day around a palm-sized portion of a lean protein you like. | Protein-anchored meal ≥6/7 days? | Protein is the highest-leverage recomp nutrient; anchoring one meal is near-zero friction and rides straight off the yogurt habit. |
> | **B3 — Protein every main meal** | Extend the palm portion to every main meal — one palm women, two palms men. | Every main meal had a protein portion, ≥6/7 days? | Same skill repeated, not a new one; pushes total protein into range with no counting. |
> | **B4 — Produce volume** | Add a fist or two of vegetables or fruit to each main meal. | Every main meal had produce, ≥6/7 days? | Pure addition, not restriction — lowest-friction way to raise fiber/water/volume and start displacing dense food. |
> | **B5 — Protein + produce first** | At each main meal, eat the protein and produce before the starch/fat. | Started with protein+produce ≥6/7 days? | The deficit starts biting here: pre-loading satiety shrinks the dense remainder with nothing banned. Requires B3+B4 already live. |

>
> ## Where the deficit switches on
> **It's emergent, not a dedicated brick.** There is no "eat less" rung — you can't name a number, and restriction-first is the lowest-adherence way to build a deficit. It's manufactured by displacement in three waves:
> 1. **Ignition — B4→B5.** Produce volume plus eating-it-first make fullness arrive earlier. Same meals, less of the dense remainder eaten. Mechanical switch-on.
> 2. **Deepening — B6 + B7.** Strips the two largest discretionary buckets — liquid sugar and grazing snacks — by swapping, not banning.
> 3. **Consolidation — B8.** The one overtly portion-limiting move, applied only to starch/fat, and only once appetite is already suppressed upstream.
>
> Protein (B2/B3) doesn't create the deficit — it's the enabling condition. High protein raises satiety and thermic effect and protects lean mass, so when the deficit emerges it drives *fat* off instead of muscle. That's the whole recomp requirement in one design choice.
>
> ## Protein
> Enters at **B2**, immediately after yogurt, because it's the recomp keystone. Expressed as hand portions, never grams: **one palm per meal for women, two for men.** One palm is roughly 20–30g — hidden math, never spoken. Fully installed by **B3**: every main meal, plus the B1 yogurt, lands most beginners near the internal target (coach tracks ~1.6 g/kg actual bodyweight; use ~1.2–1.6 for high-BF clients to avoid overshoot). Reinforced a second time at **B7** via the protein-default snack, which catches clients who under-hit at meals.
>
> ## Biggest risks
> **Most likely to fail the weekly binary:**
> - **B3 (protein every meal).** Breakfast and eating out are the classic misses. Mitigation: let the yogurt tub or eggs *be* the breakfast protein so B3 demands no new cooking.
> - **B8 (no seconds).** Highest failure risk — the only restriction rung. Placed last on purpose. Expect repeat weeks. That's the design working, not breaking.
> - **B6/B9 (drinks/alcohol).** Alcohol is social and can be dependency-linked — behaviorally nothing like swapping a soda.
>
> **Secretly coupled — do NOT split:**
> - **"Protein first" and "produce first" are one skill (B5), not two.** Splitting halves the satiety mechanism and doubles the weeks for zero adherence gain.
> - **Buying is welded to eating.** Every food brick silently needs a shop/stock sub-behavior — bundle it into the same rung. No standalone "buy protein" bricks. B1 already models this.
>
> **Should be split apart:**
> - **Alcohol out of B6.** Lumping it with soda chains an easy swap to a hard social change; one failure sinks both clean weeks. Keep separate.
>
> **Evidence caveats to keep in the doc:**
> - Recomp is realistic specifically for untrained, higher-BF clients and fades as they lean out. Don't oversell.
> - Contingent on resistance training — without it, expect fat loss + muscle preservation only.
> - High protein is safe in healthy kidneys. Pre-existing kidney disease, T2D, or disordered-eating history changes this — those clients need a physician/RD gating the protein load and B8, not this ladder.
>
> **What I expect executed:** one skill per week, weekly binary, one clean week to advance, hold-and-repeat on a miss. Do not stack two rungs to "catch up." Coach tracks the hidden numbers; the client only ever gets the behavior. Check back after the first client cohort clears B5 — the first rung where the deficit is live.

Every proposed rung is instead a *daily behavior with a 6/7 tolerance* — a new forgiveness-threshold mechanic not in the plan. Needs a deliberate decision: adopt 6/7 as the standard food-brick shape, client told "every day" while the coach forgives one miss internally (consistent with never-reveal-numbers).
