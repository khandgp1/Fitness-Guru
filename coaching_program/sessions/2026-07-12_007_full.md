> Brick Two is a lean protein. Here's the menu the client picks from. One rule: he chooses ONE, buys it, eats it through the week. Same mechanics that made the yogurt stick.

> ## The menu — pick ONE
>
> **1. Tuna or chicken in a pouch (or can), packed in water**
> Shelf-stable for years. Rip it open, eat it. No fridge, no cooking, no smell if you go pouch, no draining. Stock a pile in one trip and the week's handled. ~20 g protein per 5 oz pouch, roughly $1–2 each. Buy it in **water, not oil** — plain, no flavor name on the front.
>
> **2. Canned/pouched pre-cooked chicken breast**
> Same shelf-stable, open-and-eat profile as tuna, for people who don't want fish. ~15–20 g per pouch, ~$2–3. Plain breast in water only — skip the teriyaki/sesame/buffalo versions.
>
> **3. Pre-cooked grilled chicken strips (frozen bag)**
> Tastes like a real meal, not a snack. Microwave 60–90 seconds or eat cold. Frozen means zero spoilage clock — one bag kills all re-sourcing pressure for the week. ~20 g per 3 oz, ~$5–7 a bag. Buy **"grilled," never "breaded" or "crispy."**
>
> **4. Rotisserie chicken**
> Highest palatability on the board — feels like dinner, not diet food. One grab covers several days. ~25 g per palm-sized breast portion, $5–8 for the bird. **Pull the skin off** to keep it lean. Only catch: 3–4 days fridge life, so it may need one mid-week restock.
>
> **5. Pre-peeled hard-boiled eggs (2-packs)**
> Open and eat. No cooking, no peeling. ~6 g each, ~$1–2 a pack, keeps about a week. Whole egg so not ultra-lean, but nutrient-dense and completely fine for recomp.
>
> **6. Pre-packaged deli turkey breast (the sealed shelf packs, NOT the deli counter)**
> No counter wait, sealed pack keeps 5–7 days, eat by the slice. ~10–12 g per couple slices, $4–6. This is where sodium and junk additives hide — pick the lowest-sodium pack and avoid anything labeled "turkey product."
>
> **7. Low-fat cottage cheese (1% or 2%, plain)**
